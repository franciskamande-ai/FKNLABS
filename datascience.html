Models & Performance

Gradient Boosted Decision Trees (XGBoost / LightGBM / CatBoost)

Gradient Boosted Decision Trees (GBMs) are one of the most widely used machine learning methods in finance and economics. üîπ Why it works on macro + market data: Nonlinear relationships: Markets react differently depending on conditions (e.g., unemployment rising during inflation is treated differently than during deflation). GBMs can capture such nonlinear interactions naturally. Feature importance: GBMs let us measure which indicators (NFP, CPI, GDP, rates) are most predictive for returns, volatility, or trends. Robustness: Handles missing data, categorical encodings, and noisy signals much better than linear regression. Speed & efficiency: LightGBM, for example, is extremely fast even with millions of rows. üîπ Limitations: GBMs are not time-aware by default ‚Üí they treat inputs as independent rows. For time-series like OHLCV or yield curves, we must create lagged features (t-1, t-2 returns), rolling averages, volatility windows, macro event flags, etc. üîπ Example usage in your datasets: Predicting the next day‚Äôs direction of EURUSD using last 30 days of CPI, GDP, and OHLCV features. Estimating how much NFP surprises impact yields by using historical macro surprises + rate policy shifts. üëâ GBM should be your baseline model ‚Äî stable, interpretable, and surprisingly strong.

Recurrent Neural Networks (LSTM / GRU / TFT)

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) ‚Äî especially LSTMs (Long Short-Term Memory) and GRUs (Gated Recurrent Units) ‚Äî are designed for sequential data, which makes them powerful for financial time series and macroeconomic forecasting. üîπ Why it works on macro + market data: Captures memory: Economic effects are delayed. A rate hike today affects unemployment months later. LSTMs can model this sequential dependency. Handles multiple series: You can feed OHLCV + macro indicators into a single model to learn interactions over time. Flexible forecasting horizons: LSTMs can be adapted for single-step (next day) or multi-step (next quarter GDP) forecasting. Advanced extensions: Transformers (like the Temporal Fusion Transformer, TFT) combine the interpretability of GBMs with the sequence-handling of RNNs ‚Üí currently state-of-the-art. üîπ Limitations: Data-hungry: Needs lots of history and careful tuning. Prone to overfitting on financial data if not regularized (dropout, weight decay, cross-validation). Slower to train compared to GBMs. üîπ Example usage in your datasets: Forecasting short-term FX volatility by feeding past OHLCV + macro events into an LSTM. Predicting quarterly GDP growth using sequences of inflation, rates, and employment figures. Combining deep learning with event embeddings (like ‚ÄúNFP release‚Äù as a feature) to capture how markets digest news. üëâ LSTM/GRU (or TFT) should be your advanced research model ‚Äî capturing dynamic time effects that tree models can‚Äôt..
